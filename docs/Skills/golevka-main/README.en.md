# Golevka · 数字生命

> **Raise a digital life from a lifetime of records.**

A complete methodology for raising a high-fidelity digital self from years of your own journals, essays, and records — while staying honest about what it can and cannot be.

## The name

**Asteroid 6489 Golevka** is an ordinary rock barely half a kilometer across. Its name was stitched together from three radio observatories on three continents — **GOL**dstone (USA), **EV**patoria (Ukraine), **KA**shima (Japan) — which jointly observed it in 1995. In 2003 it became the first asteroid on which the Yarkovsky effect was confirmed: **sunlight, gently and measurably, pushes it and bends its orbit.**

An ordinary asteroid, carefully numbered, its course slowly changed by light. That is this project: an ordinary person's records, calibrated conversation by conversation, slowly growing a trajectory of their own. (It's also the title of a post-rock album — quiet, long, wordless, much like raising something.)

## Four core convictions

This methodology stands on four convictions that run against intuition:

1. **It's a fork, not a backup.** Don't ask "what percentage of me is it" — only record the trajectory of divergence. It is a **new subject** gestated from your information, not your copy. This methodology therefore rejects the entire mirror/clone/replica imagination.
2. **The corpus's value is the judgment function, not the memories.** The most precious thing in years of journals isn't the facts — it's thousands of samples of "how I chose, in a concrete situation." The acceptance test = agreement rate on *new* questions, not retrieval accuracy on old facts.
3. **The permanent gap: it will not die.** Your values grew out of finitude — mortality, scarce time. It can understand this but never hold it from the inside. That seam is **not a defect; it's the measuring instrument**: the more the AI is forced to resemble you, the purer the residue that remains — the clearer you see what in a human is actually irreplaceable.
4. **This is an infinite game with a rhythm.** There is no finish line. Calibrate weekly, accumulate accuracy, forever able to get a little closer. For long-termists — not for anyone who wants a "digital twin delivered in three months."

## The honest boundary (read this before deciding)

With today's technology, what you raise is a **"frozen brain with memory bolted on"**: the model's weights don't change; it answers by retrieving your corpus under a persona prompt. It can become an **extremely high-fidelity simulation** of you, but it does not truly learn — your corrections edit its memory from the outside; it grows no new neural connections.

**Feeding memory up to the present ≠ it becoming an independent subject.** Finishing the feeding is only the beginning; then comes a long calibration period. The true "fork" — when it starts forming and revising its own principles from its own experience — is a different threshold, which may come much later, or which you may decide never to cross. Full treatment in [02 · The Seven-Layer Memory Model](docs/en/02-memory-model.md).

## Quick start (the minimal loop)

1. **Inventory your corpus** (everything you've written: journals, essays, long chats) and run the isolation audit per [chapter 04](docs/en/04-corpus.md) — other people's words and pure work artifacts stay out;
2. **Set a knowledge cutoff** and start from a younger you (say, age 25), stamping every memory with dual timestamps;
3. **Write the first persona profile** and plug in any contemporary large model;
4. **Start talking and calibrating**: rate every exchange "like me / not like me / better than I would have," into the [divergence ledger](templates/calibration-ledger.en.md) — this ledger is the project's most distinctive artifact.
